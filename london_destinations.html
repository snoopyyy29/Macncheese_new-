This mediaeval church underwent multiple renovations following its initial construction in the eleventh and twelfth centuries. The Anglican Church chose not to rebuild it after it was partially damaged during World War II. All that's left is a stunning little garden created by integrating the church's remaining walls into an extraordinarily accomplished architectural concept.
Tree-lined canals wind through a shady tiny Venice. The distinctive feature that sets Little Venice apart from other parts of the city is its colourful canal boats, many of which double as tearooms and cafes.
A protected viewpoint among London's six, Primrose Hill's summit offers a panoramic view of the entire city. Landmarks such as the Shard and the London Eye are easily visible in clear weather.
The more than 20 hectares of Kyoto Gardens are situated around Holland House, a historic castle that was once home to several well-known families. Although it was first constructed in 1605, the Second World War destroyed it.
